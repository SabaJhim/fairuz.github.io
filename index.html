About

I build machine-learning systems that stay trustworthy when data is private, distributed, and safety-critical.

I am a Lecturer in Computer Science and Engineering at Presidency University and a recent B.Sc. graduate of the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), where I finished with a CGPA of 3.94/4.00. My work sits at the intersection of privacy-preserving machine learning and system reliability: I study how collaborative learning can leak information, how models can be made to reliably forget, and how autonomous systems can be tested against realistic behavior.

I am motivated by problems where technical rigor meets real-world consequence — data confidentiality, algorithmic safety, and equitable access to technology — and I aim to contribute both through research and by building a collaborative academic environment for students.

Research Experience

Label Inference Attacks in Vertical Federated Learning

2025 – Present

Undergraduate Thesis · BUET (Supervisor: Dr. Muhammad Abdullah Adnan)

  • Investigating security vulnerabilities in Vertical Federated Learning (VFL) under semi-honest participants, focusing on label inference and gradient inversion attacks.
  • Developed SBEA, an attack exploiting set bias in entity alignment for label inference and model extraction; manuscript submitted to IJCAI 2026.
  • Designing and evaluating defenses that preserve label privacy while maintaining model utility.

Machine Unlearning in Vertical Federated Learning

2026 – Present

Independent Research · BUET

  • Studying machine unlearning in the VFL setting, where feature partitioning across parties makes standard (horizontal) unlearning methods inapplicable.
  • Establishing a standardized evaluation metric and benchmark protocol that quantifies unlearning completeness, residual information leakage, and post-unlearning utility.
  • Benchmarking retraining, gradient-based, and approximate unlearning strategies for fair, reproducible comparison across VFL architectures.

Simulation-Based Testing of Autonomous Vehicles

2024 – Present

Voluntary Research · BUET × Cal Poly × UC Santa Cruz

  • Enhancing the reliability of simulation-based testing frameworks for autonomous vehicles, in collaboration with BUET, California Polytechnic State University, and UC Santa Cruz.
  • Developing metrics for the diversity and realism of pedestrian trajectories so test scenarios better reflect real-world behavior.
  • Built and deployed a public trajectory annotation website to collect human realism judgments for metric validation.
