About

Building trustworthy ML systems

I work at the intersection of privacy, security, and machine learning — studying how collaborative models can be made to both learn and forget responsibly.

I am a Lecturer in Computer Science and Engineering at Presidency University and a recent B.Sc. graduate of the Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), where I finished with a CGPA of 3.94/4.00. My research focuses on privacy-preserving machine learning and system reliability: how collaborative learning can leak information, how models can be made to reliably forget, and how autonomous systems can be tested against realistic behaviour.

I am motivated by problems where technical rigour meets real-world consequence — data confidentiality, algorithmic safety, and equitable access to technology — and I aim to contribute both through research and by building a collaborative academic environment for students.
