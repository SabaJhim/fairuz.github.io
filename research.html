Academic Work

Research

Studying how machine-learning systems can be made trustworthy when data is private, distributed, and safety-critical.

Focus Areas

Research Interests

My work spans privacy-preserving machine learning, federated systems security, and reliable autonomous systems. Below are the active threads.

Vertical Federated Learning Privacy & Security in ML Label Inference Attacks Gradient Inversion Machine Unlearning Trustworthy AI Autonomous-Vehicle Testing Simulation & Robustness Gender Bias in LLMs Fairness & Interpretability

Motivation

Research Philosophy

I am drawn to problems at the boundary of technical rigour and real-world consequence. Data-collaborative learning is reshaping how AI is built across organisations and jurisdictions — yet the privacy contracts it implies are rarely made explicit, let alone enforced. I want to close that gap.

Similarly, as autonomous systems move into public spaces, the gap between simulation performance and real-world behaviour can be life-critical. Building better evaluation infrastructure for these systems is as important as improving the models themselves.

Active Work

Research Projects

Label Inference Attacks in Vertical Federated Learning

2025 – Present

Undergraduate Thesis · BUET — Supervisor: Dr. Muhammad Abdullah Adnan

  • Investigating security vulnerabilities in Vertical Federated Learning (VFL) under semi-honest participants, focusing on label inference and gradient inversion attacks.
  • Developed SBEA, a novel attack exploiting set bias in entity alignment for label inference and model extraction; manuscript submitted to IJCAI 2026.
  • Designing and evaluating defences that preserve label privacy while maintaining model utility across realistic VFL architectures.

Machine Unlearning in Vertical Federated Learning

2026 – Present

Independent Research · BUET

  • Studying machine unlearning in the VFL setting, where feature partitioning across parties makes standard (horizontal) unlearning methods inapplicable.
  • Establishing a standardised evaluation metric and benchmark protocol that quantifies unlearning completeness, residual information leakage, and post-unlearning utility.
  • Benchmarking retraining, gradient-based, and approximate unlearning strategies for fair, reproducible comparison across VFL architectures.

Simulation-Based Testing of Autonomous Vehicles

2024 – Present

Voluntary Research · BUET × Cal Poly × UC Santa Cruz

  • Enhancing the reliability of simulation-based testing frameworks for autonomous vehicles, in collaboration with BUET, California Polytechnic State University, and UC Santa Cruz.
  • Developing metrics for the diversity and realism of pedestrian trajectories so test scenarios better reflect real-world behaviour.
  • Built and deployed a public trajectory annotation website to collect human realism judgements for metric validation.

Gender Stereotype in Large Language Models

2025 – Present

Research · EliteResearchLab LLC

  • Mini-review investigating how gender stereotypes are encoded and propagated in contemporary LLMs, and evaluating both biasing and debiasing techniques.
  • Manuscript submitted to Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence.
